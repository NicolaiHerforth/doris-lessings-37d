A house that feels
like a secret

You open the door and feel it straightaway. The space breathes. Light falls at an angle through three storeys, and you can hear the silence — the kind of silence you only find in a home designed with intention. 150 square metres across six levels, each with its own character, its own light.

Life unfolds slowly here. Coffee on the warm kitchen level. Focused work half a floor up. The children an entire level to themselves. And when the day lets go of you — the roof. Your private terrace, string lights, sunset over Ørestad. This isn't a flat. It's a house in the city — with the metro to Kongens Nytorv in twelve minutes.

Architecture

Built for living,
not just inhabiting

The Bella area has many townhouses. But only five of them — the Bella row — were designed with this split-level architecture. They rarely come to market. A townhouse of this spatial quality is nearly impossible to find at this price in Copenhagen. Each half-storey shifts the perspective — you move upward through the home as through a slow breath. Nothing repeats. Nothing feels accidental.

Location

Two minutes to the metro.
Three to nature.

Ørestad sits where the city ends and the landscape begins. The metro takes you to Kongens Nytorv in twelve minutes. Amager Fælled starts at your back door. The airport is closer than Nørrebro. You don't live on the outskirts of anything — you live in the middle of everything.

The neighbourhood

Canals, commons
and neighbours who wave

Ørestad South has the energy of a young neighbourhood that's finally found its rhythm. The canals mirror the sky each morning. The commons stretch southward like an open lung. The Bella row sits set back from the road, surrounded by low-rise buildings and shared courtyards — it feels more like a pocket of calm than a district.

Your children cycle to school along the water. You catch the metro in two minutes. And in summer — when the light hangs in the air until ten — you sit on the roof and watch the sun disappear behind the commons. Slowly. As though it doesn't want to leave either.
